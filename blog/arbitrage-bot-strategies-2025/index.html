Arbitrage Bot Strategies That Actually Work in 2025

Dec 10, 20249 min read
← Back to Blog

Arbitrage trading bots exploit price inefficiencies across exchanges and trading pairs. In 2025, with tighter spreads and faster markets, only well-engineered bots with sub-10ms execution can consistently profit.

Cross-Exchange Arbitrage

The classic strategy: buy on Exchange A where price is lower, sell on Exchange B where price is higher. Profitability depends on execution speed, transfer fees, and spread. With co-location and direct API access, execution under 5ms is achievable. Key challenge: capital must be pre-positioned on both exchanges.

Triangular Arbitrage

Exploit price discrepancies between three trading pairs on the same exchange. Example: BTC/USDT → ETH/BTC → ETH/USDT. No transfer delays, but requires fast order execution and careful fee calculation. Opportunities are fleeting — typically under 100ms.

Statistical Arbitrage

Use mean-reversion models on correlated asset pairs. When the spread between two historically correlated assets diverges beyond a threshold, take opposing positions expecting reversion. Requires backtesting on at least 2 years of tick data and robust risk management.

Flash Loan Arbitrage (DeFi)

Borrow large amounts with no collateral within a single transaction, execute arbitrage across DEXs, repay the loan — all atomically. Requires Solidity expertise and gas optimization. Profitable opportunities exist but competition from MEV bots is intense.

Risk Management

Always implement position limits, maximum drawdown stops, and circuit breakers. Monitor slippage in real-time and pause the bot if market conditions change. Never run arbitrage bots without thorough backtesting and paper trading first.
