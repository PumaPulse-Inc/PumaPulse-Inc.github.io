Layer 2 Scaling Solutions: Which One Should Your DApp Use?

Nov 1, 202413 min read
← Back to Blog

Ethereum's Layer 2 ecosystem has matured dramatically. With Optimism, Arbitrum, zkSync, and Polygon all offering different tradeoffs, choosing the right L2 for your DApp is a critical architectural decision.

Optimistic Rollups

Optimism and Arbitrum use optimistic rollups — transactions are assumed valid and only challenged if fraud is detected. 7-day withdrawal period to L1. Advantages: EVM-equivalent, easy migration from Ethereum, large existing ecosystem. Best for: DeFi protocols, NFT marketplaces, gaming.

ZK Rollups

zkSync Era and StarkNet use zero-knowledge proofs to validate transactions cryptographically. Near-instant finality, no fraud challenge period. Advantages: stronger security guarantees, faster withdrawals. Disadvantages: higher proving costs, less EVM compatibility (improving rapidly).

Polygon

Polygon PoS is a sidechain (not a true L2) with its own validator set. Polygon zkEVM is a true ZK rollup. Polygon has the largest ecosystem outside Ethereum mainnet. Best for: high-volume consumer apps, gaming, social applications where low fees matter most.

Performance Comparison

Arbitrum: ~40,000 TPS theoretical, ~$0.01-0.10 per transaction. Optimism: similar to Arbitrum. zkSync Era: ~2,000 TPS currently, scaling rapidly. StarkNet: ~1,000 TPS, improving. All are orders of magnitude cheaper than Ethereum mainnet.

Our Recommendation

For DeFi: Arbitrum (largest TVL, best liquidity). For NFTs/gaming: Polygon or Immutable X. For privacy-sensitive apps: zkSync or StarkNet. For maximum composability with Ethereum: stay on mainnet or use Optimism. Always consider where your users and liquidity already are.
