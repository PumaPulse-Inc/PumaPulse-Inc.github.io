Building a DEX in 2025: AMM vs Order Book

Nov 28, 202411 min read
← Back to Blog

Decentralized exchanges processed over $1 trillion in volume in 2024. Choosing between an AMM and order book model is the most critical architectural decision when building a DEX.

AMM Architecture

Automated Market Makers use liquidity pools and mathematical formulas (x*y=k for Uniswap v2) to price assets. No order book needed — trades execute against the pool. Advantages: always liquid, permissionless listing, passive LP income. Disadvantages: impermanent loss, high slippage for large trades.

Order Book DEX

On-chain order books (dYdX, Serum) match buyers and sellers like a CEX but settle on-chain. Advantages: better price discovery, no slippage for limit orders, familiar UX. Disadvantages: gas costs for every order, slower execution, front-running risk.

Hybrid Approaches

Uniswap v3 introduced concentrated liquidity, allowing LPs to provide liquidity in specific price ranges — effectively creating an on-chain order book from AMM positions. Hybrid DEXs like Vertex combine off-chain order matching with on-chain settlement.

Smart Contract Security

DEX smart contracts hold user funds and are immutable once deployed. Reentrancy attacks, flash loan exploits, and oracle manipulation are the most common attack vectors. Always audit with at least two reputable firms and implement circuit breakers.

Choosing Your Chain

Ethereum for maximum liquidity and composability. Solana for speed (65,000 TPS) and low fees. BNB Chain for retail users. Arbitrum/Optimism for Ethereum security with lower gas. Consider multi-chain deployment for maximum reach.
